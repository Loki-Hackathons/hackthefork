## Pitch Method

In the final phase of the event, the top 10 projects will be pitched. Each pitch lasts a maximum of 4 minutes.

- This pitch is expected to clearly present the problem being addressed and explain why it is important ;
- Teams should then describe their proposed solution, and how it differs from existing approaches. A short demo or concrete illustration (such as a prototype, interface, simulation, or user journey) should be included in order to make the solution tangible.
- Finally, teams must outline how their solution could be deployed in real-world conditions, specifying potential users, implementation steps, relevant partners, and scalability over time.

## Examples of problems

Use AI to reduce losses in menu and product creation. Losses refer to the percentage of an ingredient that is lost during the production process (for example, a certain quantity of carrots lost in a carrot-based dish).

How can AI generate the testing phase of plant-based analog products (meat, milk, cheese, etc.)?

How can AI be used to improve traceability from raw materials to finished products?

How can AI simplify ingredient lists to highlight healthier products (enhancing or improving Nutri-Score, Yuka ratings, etc.)?

How can the price of a product versus its benefits be explained clearly to consumers in order to facilitate adoption?
